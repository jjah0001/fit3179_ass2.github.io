The Power of the English Language


English is known to be the international language, mainly thanks to the British Empire. As an international language, it is historically and geographically powerful, pushing several countries to improve their English skills for Business and Tourism purposes for instance. At the same time, how far has countries pushed for to improve their English proficiency? Did some of these countries pushed 'too far'?

Geography and History

The British Empire is the largest empire in history and it is believed that its former colonies are able to speak better English than those that were never colonies. Note: The Philippines was a US colony, another English-speaking country.

Geographically, European countries generally have a stronger English proficiency while the Middle Eastern countries struggle to keep their proficiency high.

Colonialism did not play a strong role in the English proficiency of most countries. In fact, the highest scoring country The Netherlands was never a colony and on the other hand Yemen, the lowest scoring was a former colony of the British.

Culture and Politics

Certain countries may want to make English as the main language of communication. This is prevelant in several African countries such as Ghana and Nigeria as for the numerous tribal languages that exist in the country. Others, such as Singapore and Malaysia, is doing so to increase their global competitiveness.

Malaysia

While Malay is the official language of Malaysia, the government have been pushing for a wider use of English. This is to get the population in par with a more globalised world.

As seen from the video, many locals find the change hard especially if they have come from a rural area. Replacing Malay with English will also push for many locals to lose their identity and their proficiency with their native tongue. This was the case with a country which was formerly part of Malaysia, Singapore.

Singapore

While the cultures are similar in neighbouring Singapore, the economy of Singapore is more reliant on a global market. On top of that, the country consists of three main ethnic groups and the government replaced Malay with English as the main language of communication upon independence while encouraging its citizens to be proficient in their mother tongue, therefore making Mandarin, Malay and Tamil as official languages along with English.

With the government trying to encourage the use of English, the use of English at home skyrocketed as it became the most popular language spoken at home in the country while the other 3 official languages have declined in popularity, which shows the power of the English language in not only Singapore, but also in several other countries.

Film and Media Industry

With the rise of Hollywood films coming from the US as well as applications such as YouTube and Netflix, there are more means of learning English. As these films come mainly from the US, this may mean the rise of American English as opposed to British English. So you might here more people referring to 'pants' than 'trousers', or 'fries' rather than 'chips'.

Conclusion

The English Language is significantly powerful. It may have been started by the Brits, but the Americans are taking over.

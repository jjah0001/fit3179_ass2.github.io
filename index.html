The Power of the English Language


English is known to be the international language, thanks to the British Empire. As an international language, it is historically and geographically powerful, pushing several countries to improve their English skills for Business and Tourism purposes for instance. At the same time, how far has countries pushed for to improve their English proficiency? Did some of these countries pushed 'too far'?

Geography and History

The British Empire is the largest empire in history and it is believed that its former colonies are able to speak better English. Note: The Philippines was a US colony, another English-speaking country.

As seen here, most of Europe has a stronger English proficiency than most other continent. This may show how each European country are interdependent on one another or have strong links with the United Kingdom. Outside of Europe, Singapore also have a strong English proficiency mainly due to its status as a business hub while being a former British colony. (We'll touch on Singapore later)

While some former British and American colonies such as South Africa, Malaysia, Nigeria and the Philippines have high English proficiencies, most other former colonies do not, which shows how most countries are trying to move on from their colonial roots.

Business

It is believed that in the business world, English would be very important. The chart below will show the English proficiency of each country which will be ordered based on their Ease of Business rankings.

There is a general trend that the higher the English proficiency, the easier it is to conduct business in the country. It is important for governments to communicate their needs and wants efficiently and proficiency in English, as an international language, would be extremely important.

Financial Centres such as Singapore and Hong Kong had made English as their official language, despite Hong Kong's proficiency score being classified as moderate. There are also some countries such as Thailand or Rwanda which has scored relatively low than most countries of similar rank.

Tourism

As an international language, it is expected that with more tourists, the higher the proficiency. Countries may want to up their English proficiencies in order to communicate better with tourists.

From the scattered chart, there seems to be barely any correlations between English proficiency and tourism. While English-speaking countries such as the US, UK, India and Australia are a top-source of tourists, non-English speaking countries such as China are taking over, making English as a less-dominant language in terms of tourism.

Certain countries such as Thailand have a concerningly low proficiency rate but is still able to attract a large number of tourists.

Culture and Politics

Certain countries may want to make English as the main language of communication. This is prevelant in several African countries such as Ghana and Nigeria as for the numerous tribal languages that exist in the country. Others, such as Singapore and Malaysia, is doing so to increase their global competitiveness.

While Malay is the official language of Malaysia, the government have been pushing for a wider use of English. This is to get the population in par with a more globalised world.

As seen from the video, many locals find the change hard especially if they have come from a rural area. Replacing Malay with English will also push for many locals to lose their identity and their proficiency with their native tongue. This was the case with a country which was formerly part of Malaysia, Singapore.

While the cultures are similar in neighbouring Singapore, the economy of Singapore is more reliant on a global market. On top of that, the country consists of three main ethnic groups and the government replaced Malay with English as the main language of communication upon independence while encouraging its citizens to be proficient in their mother tongue, therefore making Mandarin, Malay and Tamil as official languages along with English.

With the government trying to encourage the use of English, the use of English at home skyrocketed as it became the most popular language spoken at home in the country while the other 3 official languages have declined in popularity, which shows the power of the English language in not only Singapore, but also in several other countries.
